# Device_usage_trend_analysis

This case study analyzes Bellabeat customer usage data to better understand how consumers interact with the company's products. The objective of this analysis is to identify meaningful trends and generate actionable insights that can support the development of effective marketing campaigns.
Business task: Understanding trends thanks to user data and establish recommendations for new marketing campaigns.

Stakeholders

BellaBeat marketing team 
Product development team
Executive leadership

Data sources

Data set name: FitBit Fitness Tracker Data

Source: Kaggle

Files used: 

dailyActivity_merged.csv
hourlySteps_merged.csv
sleepDay_merged.csv

Dataset includes: 

Daily Step Counts
Calories burned
Sleep duration
Activity intensity levels
User IDs

Data cleaning and preparation
Cleaning steps performed using SQL in Google BigQuery:
Duplicate records verification
Removed duplicates from the sleep data table
Checked column data types
Modified date formats in Sheets
Joined activity and sleep datasets in BigQuery
Data Analysis

Concepts explored: 

What is the average daily step count per user?
How does sleep duration vary across users?
How many calories users burn on average? 
Top 10 most active users and the effect on their sleep quality
Amount of users not using the device when sleeping


Key Findings

The data of Bellabeat users provided relevant insights and patterns:

Users with a high daily step count are usually shown to get more sleep than most users. There is a correlation between achieving high activity levels and sleep length.

Most users are active and using their products according to the data.
38% are not using their products when sleeping therefore they are losing on the full benefits the products have to offer. It could be due to limited feature awareness or users finding a discomfort when wearing the products to sleep in.

Most users are reaching 2000 calories burned per day which is more than the average individual. 

Some users are under 8000 steps, we can see the top performers are way over that number therefore some users could be more active to get the full benefits of the product

Recommendations

Recommendation 1: Ensure users are aware of the sleep tracking feature

38% of users are likely not using the device when sleeping which means they are losing out on the sleep tracking benefits of the devices. There is a possibility that these users are not aware of the feature. 

The new marketing campaigns could include more information on the sleep-tracking devices (through videos, e-mails or texts). Notifications directly in the app could also be useful as a reminder for users that the feature exists. 

The other theory would be that the users are not wearing the products at night because of discomfort. The product development team could create an add-on or accessory of the existing Bellabeat products specifically to wear at night. The product development team could also come up with an entirely new product made specifically for comfort. 


Recommendation 2: Help users reach their goals

Tailoring a specific sport profile customizable by the user would be relevant and encourage user activity. The profile would be adapted to the goals communicated in-app by the user and would send in-app reminders according to the schedule chosen. 

Developing rewards and gamifying the activity process could encourage users to get more from their devices by getting their steps in. The users would get points when reaching their step goals. 
We could create a new feature so the users can add friends and also get points when referring a new customer. The points could then be used to customize their own avatar in the app and compete with their network. 

This would boost activity but also encourage existing users to talk about the devices around them. 


Recommendation 3: Provide sleep statistics linked to activity 

Providing users with a report of their monthly activities and their quality of sleep would encourage users to use both physical tracking features. Creating marketing campaigns showing the correlation between sleep and activity levels would motivate users to get more steps in and to use their Bellabeat products. 

The marketing campaign should be by e-mail or videos to truly explain the stats behind the concept of sleeping better when moving more. 


Recommendation 4: Highlight the results of users when developing new marketing campaigns 

Most app and Bellabeat device users are burning a good amount of calories, proving that the products are efficient. This could interest many potential users still debating on the need of the product in their daily activities. 

Showing in the next campaigns that most users are burning 2000 calories on average is a great way to show how the products are a true help to daily health.
